Binosto reduces the risk of spine and hip fractures. Why have I been prescribed Binosto? Your doctor has prescribed Binosto (Alendronate) to treat your&nbsp; osteoporosis . Binosto (Alendronate) reduces the risk of spine and hip fractures. How does it work? Binosto (Alendronate) belongs to a group of non-hormonal medicines called bisphosphonates. Binosto (Alendronate) prevents the loss of bone that occurs in women after they have been through the menopause, and helps to rebuild bone. It reduces the risk of spine and hip fractures. When and how do I take it? Binosto (Alendronate) is taken once a week. Follow these instructions carefully to make sure you will benefit from Binosto (Alendronate). Choose the day of the week that best fits your schedule. Every week, take one effervescent tablet on your chosen day. It is very important to follow the instructions to help the Binosto (Alendronate) tablet's reach your stomach quickly and help reduce the chance of irritating your gullet (oesophagus - the tube that connects your mouth with your stomach). After getting up for the day and before taking any food, drink , or other medicine, swallow your Binosto (Alendronate) effervescent&nbsp;tablet &nbsp;dissolved in a full glass of water only (not mineral water) (not less than 200 ml or 7 fl. oz.). Do not take with mineral water (still or sparkling). Do not take with coffee or tea. Do not take with juice or milk. Do not crush or chew the tablet or allow it to dissolve in your mouth. Do not lie down — stay fully upright (sitting, standing or walking) — for at least 30 minutes after swallowing the tablet. Do not lie down until after your first food of the day. Do not take Binosto (Alendronate)&nbsp;at bedtime or before getting up for the day. If you develop difficulty or pain upon swallowing, chest pain, or new or worsening heartburn, stop taking Binosto (Alendronate) and contact your doctor. After swallowing your Binosto (Alendronate) tablet, wait at least 30 minutes before taking your first food, drink, or other medicine of the day, including antacids, calcium supplements and vitamins. Binosto (Alendronate) is effective only if taken when your stomach is empty. What’s the dose? One 70mg effervescent tablet once a week. Could it interact with other tablets? It is likely that calcium supplements, antacids, and some oral medicines will interfere with the absorption of Binosto (Alendronate) if taken at the same time.&nbsp; Please tell your doctor or pharmacist if you are taking or have recently taken any other medicines, including medicines obtained without a prescription. Herbal supplements should be used with caution and only after informing your doctor first. What are the possible risks or side-effects? Like all medicines, Binosto (Alendronate) can cause side effects, although not everybody gets them. Common side effects include: heartburn difficulty swallowing pain upon swallowing ulceration of the gullet (oesophagus - the tube that connects your mouth with your stomach) which can cause&nbsp; chest pain heartburn or difficulty or pain upon swallowing bone, muscle and/or joint pain abdominal pain; uncomfortable feeling in the stomach or belching after eating;&nbsp; constipation ; full or bloated feeling in the stomach;&nbsp; diarrhoea ; flatulence. headache. Can I drink alcohol while taking it? There are no known interactions between alcohol and Binosto (Alendronate). Always ask your doctor/pharmacist however as this may depend on what other tablets you are taking. What if I’m pregnant/breastfeeding? Binosto (Alendronate) should not be used during pregnancy or while breast feeding. If you have any more questions please ask your Pharmacist.
