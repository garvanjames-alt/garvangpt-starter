Flu vaccine — quick FAQ
• What it does: Helps your immune system recognise influenza viruses and reduce the risk of severe flu.
• Common temporary side effects: Sore arm, mild fever, tiredness, headache, muscle aches. Usually resolve within 1–3 days.
• You cannot get flu from the vaccine: The vaccine does not contain live influenza that can cause flu illness.
• Timing: Best given before flu season; it’s still useful later.
• Who should check with a clinician first: People with a history of severe allergy to any vaccine ingredient, or who had a serious reaction to a previous flu vaccine.
• Red-flags → seek urgent care: Trouble breathing, swelling of face/tongue, hives with dizziness or fainting, high fever that doesn’t settle.
• Notes on expectations: Full protection takes about two weeks.
• Education only: This is general information, not personal medical advice. Decisions should be made with a healthcare professional.
